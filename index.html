Answer key:

1. What kind of disorder is Autism?

Autism is classified as a developmental disorder because it impacts the way the brain develops and functions. It often results in characteristics such as social deficits, repetitive behaviors ("stimming"), hypersensitivity to light, textures, sounds, etc., and verbal communication issues. Every individual with Autism is affected by it differently; some people are more on the high-functioning side of the spectrum, whereas others may be on the lower end of the spectrum and need assistance doing day-to-day tasks. Thus, another name for it is Autism Spectrum Disorder.

2. How is Autism treated?

Autism is usually treated using a variety of methods, including (but not limited to) behavioral therapy, medication, occupational therapy, and speech therapy. Autism does not manifest in the same way for everyone, thus treatments can vary depending on the individual's needs. The earlier treatment is started, the bigger impact it is more likely to make, especially with treatment forms such as behavioral therapy.
